10 quick tests to measure your site’s accessibility

Toufic Sbeiti, Manager of Web Operations at Citizenship and Immigration Canada, will present 10 tests that can be performed quickly to measure the accessibility of a web page. If you are a developer, you can fix issues before posting live. If you are a project manager or a client, you can ensure the level of accessibility without advanced coding knowledge.

What to bring: Bring a laptop (FireFox with Wave and Web Developer toolbars) and follow the steps to test your web page during the presentation.

Toufic Sbeiti

Manager - Web Operations, Citizenship and Immigration Canada

Audience: Developer

Level: Beginner

Language: English

ATAG & Drupal 8

The Future of Accessible Content is going to be ATAG 2.0. A standard that both ensures that the editing interface is accessible as well as that there are measures to see that content that is produced with it is accessible. Although Drupal 8 isn't out, and ATAG 2.0 is still in draft, looking ahead it is important to anticipate what impact this will have for government sites. We can highlight enhancements to the admin side of Drupal 8, talk about the current Web Experience Toolkit Drupal implementation and what it has already adopted from Drupal 8's approach.

What to bring: Bring your laptop with a modern web browser installed.

How to migrate to Web Experience Toolkit version 4

There are major changes between earlier versions and version 4 of the Web Experience Toolkit. Migrating a website or web application to version 4 of the Toolkit is not as simple as pointing to the new Toolkit code. As part of the Government of Canada's Web Renewal Initiative, the Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development Canada migrated the Travel.gc.ca website to version 4 of the Toolkit. Join us for a discussion on the migration process and, more broadly, impacts and decisions required for content development, organization and management.

Michel Raymond

How to run a Personas workshop

This afternoon-long interactive session (aka the “Personas Workshop Workshop”) is intended to teach participants to run their own Personas Workshop, to better understand their project stakeholders and use cases. The session will teach participants the fundamentals to running a personas workshop by having them perform some of the activities that they would lead in their own sessions. Topics covered will include: when/ why to run a personas workshop, maximizing the value of the workshop, activities, prep, facilitation and follow-on activities.

What to bring: All materials will be provided. Just bring your interest in learning and your sense of fun!

Tanya Snook

Senior Business Analyst, Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat

Audience: Anyone

Level: Any

Language: English

Information architecture 101

Information Architecture (IA) 101 will review the basics of information architecture including what information architecture is (and is not), what an information architecture looks like, what areas information architecture covers, what a strong information architecture does and why it’s important.

We’ll also look at and discuss information architecture in the context of large scale, complex systems like the Government of Canada’s website and news sources like the BBC. This session will be filled with great examples, tips, resources and chance to talk about how information architecture can help build a better Web.

What to bring: An open mind and a desire to learn about the magic of getting the right information to the people who need it.

jp Simbandumwe

Partner, Senior Information Architect and UX Lead, Jumping Elephants
